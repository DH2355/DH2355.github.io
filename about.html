About Houston Zoo

Learn about our history, mission, and what makes us one of the most visited zoos in America.

Our History

The Houston Zoo opened its gates in 1922, starting with a small collection of animals housed in Sam Houston Park. Over the decades, the zoo has grown into one of the most visited zoological parks in the United States, welcoming over 2.5 million guests each year.

In the 1940s, the zoo relocated to its current 55-acre home within Hermann Park, a beloved urban oasis. Major renovations in the 2000s brought world-class exhibits like the African Forest, the McNair Asian Elephant Habitat, and the award-winning Galápagos Islands exhibit.

Today, the Houston Zoo is committed to connecting communities with animals to inspire action to save wildlife. Through conservation programs across six continents, the zoo actively protects over 40 species in the wild.

Key Attractions & Highlights

From majestic elephants to playful sea lions, there's something incredible around every corner.

🦍

African Forest

Home to gorillas, chimpanzees, and other African primates in lush, immersive habitats.

🐘

Asian Elephant Habitat

A state-of-the-art home for our Asian elephants with pools, mud wallows, and enrichment areas.

🐢

Galápagos Islands

Walk through an immersive island ecosystem with giant tortoises, blue-footed boobies, and more.

🐦

Texas Wetlands

Discover native Texas wildlife, including bald eagles, alligators, and whooping cranes.

🌊

Sea Lion Show

Watch our California sea lions perform and learn about marine conservation.

🎠

Wildlife Carousel

Ride hand-carved endangered species on this beautifully crafted carousel for all ages.
